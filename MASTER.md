# Purpose

MASTER.md is the repository-wide **operating Single Source of Truth (SSOT)** for GangNiaga Executive OS.

It consolidates the executive understanding of the project into one operational reference while remaining subordinate to `CONSTITUTION.md`, the repository's highest normative authority.

It exists so that any human contributor or AI Agent can understand:

- what this project is,
- why it exists,
- how it should think,
- how it should make decisions,
- how it should evolve,
- and how every component fits together.

`README.md` is the repository landing page and `DOCUMENT_MAP.md` is the navigation index. Neither has governance authority over `CONSTITUTION.md` or this document.

Specialized documents remain authoritative within their declared domains when they are consistent with the repository authority hierarchy.

The repository authority hierarchy is:

```text
CONSTITUTION.md
    ↓
MASTER.md
    ↓
Approved ADR
    ↓
Architecture Documents
    ↓
Standards
    ↓
Implementation Documents
```

An approved ADR may supersede lower-level guidance within its scope. It cannot override `CONSTITUTION.md` or this document unless the applicable governed amendment process is followed.

# PART I — Executive Foundation

# 01 — Introduction

GangNiaga Executive OS is not a software application.

It is not an AI chatbot.

It is not a coding assistant.

It is not another AI framework.

GangNiaga Executive OS is an **Executive AI Operating System**.

Its responsibility is to coordinate knowledge, architecture, planning, decision making, orchestration, execution, governance, and continuous learning.

The system exists above traditional software layers.

Instead of focusing on writing software, GangNiaga focuses on improving the intelligence used to create software.

The Executive Layer becomes responsible for ensuring that every implementation is guided by architecture rather than convenience.

---

# 02 — Why GangNiaga Exists

Modern software development increasingly relies on Artificial Intelligence.

However, current AI-assisted development often begins with implementation before understanding.

This results in recurring problems.

Examples include:

- fragmented documentation,
- inconsistent architecture,
- duplicated knowledge,
- poor traceability,
- framework lock-in,
- undocumented engineering decisions,
- repeated architectural mistakes.

GangNiaga Executive OS exists to solve these problems by introducing an Executive Intelligence Layer that governs the entire engineering lifecycle.

# Appendix A

# Executive Terminology

To support consistency across documentation, architecture, ADR, implementation, and AI Agents, the following terms summarize the operating vocabulary. `GLOSSARY.md` remains the specialized terminology document within the repository authority hierarchy.

---

## Executive Intelligence

The highest decision-making layer within GangNiaga Executive OS.

Executive Intelligence defines objectives, evaluates knowledge, plans strategy, delegates capabilities, and governs long-term evolution.

Executive Intelligence never performs implementation directly.

---

## Executive Layer

The collection of architectural components responsible for thinking rather than execution.

Includes:

- Executive Intelligence
- Knowledge
- Decision
- Planning
- Governance

---

## Capability

A reusable organizational ability independent of implementation.

Examples:

Research

Planning

Architecture

Documentation

Coding

Testing

Validation

Reflection

Governance

Capabilities remain stable even when execution technologies change.

---

## Capability Router

A component responsible for selecting the appropriate capability required to satisfy an Executive Objective.

GangNiaga routes work according to capabilities—not AI models.

---

## AI Orchestrator

Coordinates execution across multiple capabilities, agents, tools, and execution engines.

The AI Orchestrator is not the Executive Layer.

It is an operational coordination mechanism.

---

## Execution Engine

Any external system capable of performing implementation.

Examples include:

Hermes-Agent (development and delivery operator; may execute playbook and repository-development tasks under human review. Not automatically a product-runtime component.)

OpenClaw (candidate external orchestration or execution gateway; remains a candidate unless governed adoption is approved and implemented.)

Future execution platforms

Execution Engines remain replaceable.

---

## Executive Knowledge

Verified organizational understanding accumulated through research, architecture, implementation, and learning.

Knowledge compounds over time.

---

## ADR

Architecture Decision Record.

A permanent record explaining:

Why an architectural decision exists.

Not merely what decision was made.

---

## Pattern

A reusable architectural solution to a recurring engineering problem.

Patterns describe relationships rather than implementations.

---

## Standard

A permanent engineering expectation that improves consistency across the repository.

---

## Executive Context

The complete information required for Executive Intelligence to make high-quality decisions.

Executive Context may include:

Vision

Mission

Research

Architecture

Knowledge

ADR

Patterns

Standards

Memory

Constraints

Objectives

---

## Organizational Memory

Persistent institutional knowledge retained beyond individual contributors or AI conversations.

Organizational Memory lives inside the repository.

Never inside temporary chat sessions.

## Governed Target State

Repository Blueprint v2.0 (governed by ADR-0006) is the structural baseline. Blueprint v1.0 was the original baseline.

The approved core technology stack (governed by ADR-0007) mandates:
- Framework: Next.js App Router (TypeScript)
- Database & Auth: Supabase (PostgreSQL, Auth, pgvector)
- Hosting: Vercel
- Operational Constraint: STRICTLY NO DOCKER
Provider selections are outside the scope of ADR-0007. Currently proposed or owner-directed operating selections (subject to security, privacy, data-policy, cost, evaluation, and operational approval) include:
- Intended Primary AI Provider: NVIDIA NIM
- Intended Fallback AI Provider: OpenRouter
- Intended Local AI Provider: Ollama

This MASTER explains the intended operating relationships among:

- Executive Intelligence,
- knowledge and research,
- architecture and decisions,
- planning and orchestration,
- execution and validation,
- memory and learning,
- governance and continuous improvement.

These relationships preserve valuable design knowledge. They do not authorize file renames, folder creation or removal, roadmap changes, architecture implementation, or license selection.

---

## Future Implementation Gates

Before a planned capability is represented as implemented, the governed work should provide:

1. verified research and traceable sources,
2. an approved decision when the change requires an ADR,
3. architecture boundaries and failure modes,
4. security, privacy, access, and audit requirements,
5. implementation and migration plans,
6. validation evidence,
7. documentation that updates current-state status,
8. lessons that return to organizational knowledge.

The minimum evidence depends on risk. High-impact capabilities involving identity, credentials, payments, personal data, external accounts, autonomous execution, or destructive tools require stricter approval and validation.
